Then and Now

According to the website Britannica,

The genre was invented by Horace Walpole, whose Castle of Otranto (1765) may be said to have founded the horror story as a legitimate literary form.

Thus, 1765 is the year that paved way for a beloved genre. Since then the genre has been adapted and given life by many authors such as Edgar Allen Poe, Stephen King, Ann Rice. And with a great novel comes movie adaptations and new films inspired by those same adaptations. Some of the best horror movies to come out that keep you in suspense and shock are: Hereditary,Us, Nightmare on Elm Street, and Identity.
